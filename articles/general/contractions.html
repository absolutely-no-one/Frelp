Contractions

Main takeaways

  • Contractions are the shortening of words and make the language flow easier
  • Unlike English, French contractions must be done when applicable
  • For contracting "de + le" and "à + le", view the following:
    • De, du, de la, des
    • Au, à la, aux

What are contractions?


Contractions are the shortening of common phrases to make a language flow better. In English, an example of a contraction would be "it's" from the two words "it is," or "you're" from "you are." French has contractions, but unlike in English they must be done where possible.
